Space X!





Bio

Space X, or more officialy titled Space Exploration Technologies Corp. was formed by the well known entrepreneur Elon Musk in 2002. The goals set by this company are to produce cost effective space fairing equipment for utility, exploration, scientific endeavours, and possible future colonization of other celestial bodies. Sourced from the latest update from Wikipedia, the following is a list of highly publicized achievements performed by Space X.

  • The first privately funded liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit (Falcon 1 flight 4—September 28, 2008)
  • The first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to put a commercial satellite in orbit (RazakSAT on Falcon 1 flight 5—July 14, 2009)
  • The first privately funded company to successfully launch, orbit, and recover a spacecraft (Dragon capsule on COTS demo flight 1—December 9, 2010)
  • The first private company to send a spacecraft to the International Space Station (Dragon C2+—May 25, 2012)
  • The first private company to send a satellite into geosynchronous orbit (SES-8 on Falcon 9 flight 7—December 3, 2013)
  • The first landing of an orbital rocket's first stage on land (Falcon 9 flight 20—December 22, 2015)
  • The first landing of an orbital rocket's first stage on an ocean platform (Falcon 9 flight 23—April 8, 2016)
  • The first relaunch and landing of a used orbital rocket stage (B1021 on Falcon 9 flight 32—March 30, 2017)[57]
  • The first controlled flyback and recovery of a payload fairing (Falcon 9 flight 32—March 30, 2017)[58]
  • The first reflight of a commercial cargo spacecraft. (Dragon C106 on CRS-11 mission—June 3, 2017)[59]
  • The first privately funded payload to escape Earth's gravity. (Elon Musk's Tesla Roadster on Falcon Heavy test flight—February 6, 2018) Simultaneous recovery of two out of three boosters.[60]
